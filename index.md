Dear Ms. Kwan,

I am a constituent in Vancouver East. I'm writing to ask you to table the attached written question on the Order Paper, directed to the Minister of Canadian Heritage with the Minister of National Revenue as secondary.

The question concerns the Canadian Cultural Property Export Review Board (CCPERB) — a federal tax-expenditure program that issues tens of millions of dollars in tax-credit certifications annually for donated art, almost entirely into the hands of the same collector class that sits on Vancouver's major development and cultural-institution boards.

Unlike most tax expenditures, CCPERB publishes no transaction-level data. There is no public registry linking donor, work, appraiser, certified value, and recipient institution. The appraisals are performed by a concentrated pool of independent and gallery-affiliated appraisers — at least three of whom currently sit on the CCPERB board itself, and one of whom (Uno Langmann Limited, Vancouver) chairs the ADAC Appraisal Committee whose sign-off triggers a single-appraisal exemption at the $50,000+ threshold. The 2018–19 *Heffel v. Canada* litigation nearly collapsed the whole regime over a parallel jurisdictional question.

In plain terms: the above-ground, state-certified version of the capital-legitimation architecture you have been examining for two decades runs through the same donor class, the same civic institutions, and the same Vancouver geography — but with a federal tax credit stamped on it instead of a casino cheque. The map below the surface has been built by the Cullen Commission. The map above the surface — the CCPERB layer — has not.

The attached question is narrowly drawn to surface aggregate and structural data the government already holds, and to put the non-disclosure rationale on the public record. No legislative change is required for the Minister to answer. The Order Paper mechanism simply forces the articulation.

Given the current leadership transition and the party's focus on rebuilding around structural-inequality files, I believe this question is unusually well-suited to your voice and timing. I'd welcome the chance to discuss it further, in person at the East 10th constituency office or by whatever format suits you.

Thank you.

Neal J Moignard

## Order Paper Question — Canadian Heritage / National Revenue

With respect to certifications issued by the Canadian Cultural Property Export Review Board (CCPERB) under the *Cultural Property Export and Import Act* in fiscal years 2015–16 through 2024–25:

**(a)** For each certification, what was the certified fair market value, the recipient institution, and the fiscal year of certification?

**(b)** What was the name of each appraiser who provided an appraisal in support of each certification?

**(c)** In how many certifications did an appraiser who is also a current or former member of CCPERB provide the appraisal, and in how many did an appraiser affiliated with a commercial art gallery do so?

**(d)** In how many certifications was the appraisal provided by a committee of the Art Dealers Association of Canada (ADAC) or the National Archival Appraisal Board (NAAB) under the single-appraisal exemption available at the $50,000 threshold and above?

**(e)** What proportion of certifications in that period were associated with recipient institutions located in British Columbia?

**(f)** What is the government's policy rationale for not publishing this information in a transaction-level public registry, given that comparable federal tax expenditures are subject to transaction-level disclosure?
